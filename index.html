About Me

My name is Niko Gomez. I am currently completing a Cognitive Science major and Computer Science minor at UC Berkeley. I'm also a game developer. I started making games with Game Maker back in 5th grade and continued through high school to teach myself the essentials (sound, 2D/3D art, animation, programming, design, etc.). Although I've worked with a variety of engines and digital tools, my area of greatest proficiency and expertise is as a Unity Developer, which I took on in a professional capacity at Radd3 from october 2014 through november 2015.

Projects

Personal


I created Song Bird, a VR + Leap Motion experience, in collaboration with musician Jonathan Quartin. In it you fly across a procedural cityscape chasing songs. It debuted at the 2014 Bay Area Maker Faire.


This is a tool for generating flora by specification of growth variables such as size and color. It began as Project Ecopoesis; a game my team entered at the SF NeuroGaming Hackathon 2014. I later expanded on it's feature set, allowing specifications through animation curves.


I built this Risk game for the War Games class taught by students at UC Berkeley. In it 4 armies fight for supremacy across a series of competitions (think Ender's Game).


This is Stratos; a team deathmatch shooter in which players pilot ships and jump between planets in orbit. My goal with Stratos was to master Unity's built-in networking solution.

@Radd3


DimeTime is a VR football training simulator for the HTC Vive. I developed editor tools for designing plays and UI/UX for play selection.


Mattel's Underwater Adventure was a game designed for their new Viewmaster (think Google Cardboard). I worked primarily on sound design but also on game programming and UI/UX.


SkyIsland was my final project while working at Radd3. It's an endless runner for the GearVR. Though never released it none the less is my proudest work. I did the bulk of the programming, game design, and sound design.
